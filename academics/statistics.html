Statistics & Probability Theory

Theoretical foundations and practical applications of statistical methods

Probability Theory

My work in statistics covers both theoretical foundations and practical applications. This includes probability theory, statistical inference, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and experimental design. These mathematical tools are essential for data science and provide the theoretical underpinnings for machine learning and statistical modeling.

Probability theory forms the foundation of all statistical methods, providing the mathematical framework for understanding uncertainty and randomness. I study both discrete and continuous probability distributions, conditional probability, and the laws of large numbers.

Statistical Inference

Statistical inference allows us to make conclusions about populations based on sample data. I study point estimation, interval estimation, and hypothesis testing, understanding how to draw reliable conclusions from data while quantifying the uncertainty in those conclusions.

Regression Analysis

Regression analysis is one of the most important statistical tools for understanding relationships between variables. I study linear regression, multiple regression, logistic regression, and generalized linear models, understanding both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of these methods.

Experimental Design

Proper experimental design is crucial for obtaining reliable statistical conclusions. I study randomized controlled trials, factorial designs, blocking, and other design principles that help ensure experiments provide valid and interpretable results.

Bayesian Statistics

Bayesian statistics provides an alternative framework for statistical inference that incorporates prior knowledge and updates beliefs based on new data. I study Bayesian methods, including Bayesian inference, Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, and hierarchical Bayesian models.

Time Series Analysis

Time series analysis is essential for understanding data that varies over time. I study autoregressive models, moving averages, seasonal decomposition, and forecasting methods that help understand temporal patterns and make predictions about future values.

Multivariate Analysis

Many real-world problems involve multiple variables that may be correlated with each other. I study multivariate statistical methods including principal component analysis, factor analysis, cluster analysis, and multivariate regression to understand complex relationships in high-dimensional data.

About My Statistics Studies

I believe that a strong foundation in statistical theory is essential for applying statistical methods effectively in data science and research. My approach combines theoretical understanding with practical applications to solve real-world problems.
