Numerical Analysis & Computational Methods

Understanding and implementing computational methods for solving mathematical problems

Ancient Approximation Techniques

My work in numerical analysis focuses on understanding and implementing computational methods for solving mathematical problems. This includes studying ancient approximation techniques like the Babylonian method for square roots, as well as modern numerical algorithms for differential equations, optimization, and linear algebra.

The Babylonian method is particularly fascinating because it demonstrates how ancient mathematicians developed sophisticated computational techniques without modern computing power. This method converges quadratically to the square root, making it remarkably efficient even by today's standards.

Root Finding Algorithms

I study various methods for finding roots of equations, including the bisection method, Newton's method, and the secant method. Each algorithm has different convergence properties and computational requirements, making them suitable for different types of problems and computational constraints.

Numerical Integration

Numerical integration techniques are essential for solving problems where analytical solutions are not available. I work with methods like the trapezoidal rule, Simpson's rule, and adaptive quadrature to approximate definite integrals with high accuracy and efficiency.

Differential Equations

Many real-world problems are modeled using differential equations, and numerical methods are often necessary to find solutions. I study methods like Euler's method, Runge-Kutta methods, and finite difference schemes for solving ordinary and partial differential equations.

Linear Algebra & Matrix Computations

Numerical linear algebra is fundamental to many computational problems. I work with methods for solving linear systems, eigenvalue problems, and matrix factorizations like LU decomposition, QR decomposition, and singular value decomposition.

Optimization Methods

Numerical optimization is crucial for finding solutions to complex problems in engineering, economics, and machine learning. I study methods like gradient descent, Newton's method for optimization, and constrained optimization techniques to solve various optimization problems.

About My Numerical Analysis Work

I'm fascinated by the intersection of mathematics and computation, particularly how ancient methods remain relevant in modern numerical analysis. My approach combines theoretical understanding with practical implementation to solve real-world problems.
