Babylonian Approximation

How irrational square roots were found before calculators

  • 5 minutes Read

Over 3,000 years ago, the Babylonians devised an incredibly accurate method to approximate the square root of 2—long before modern computers. Using an iterative technique, they achieved remarkable precision in an era when such accuracy seemed unnecessary. This method, a precursor to algorithms still in use today, highlights their mathematical ingenuity and deserves further exploration.
