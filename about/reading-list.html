Reading List & Learning

Books, articles, and resources that shape my thinking

Current Reading

I'm an avid reader and lifelong learner, constantly exploring new ideas and perspectives. My reading spans mathematics, statistics, data science, business analytics, and beyond.

Data Science & Analytics

  • "The Signal and the Noise" by Nate Silver - Essential reading on prediction and forecasting
  • "The Visual Display of Quantitative Information" by Edward Tufte - Masterclass in data visualization
  • "An Introduction to Statistical Learning" by James, Witten, Hastie & Tibshirani
  • "The Elements of Statistical Learning" by Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman

Mathematics & Statistics

  • "Real Analysis" by Royden - Rigorous mathematical foundations
  • "Numerical Analysis" by Burden & Faires - Computational methods
  • "All of Statistics" by Wasserman - Comprehensive statistical theory
  • "Probability Theory" by Jaynes - Bayesian perspective

Business & Strategy

  • "Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman - Behavioral economics insights
  • "Moneyball" by Michael Lewis - Data-driven decision making in sports
  • "The Black Swan" by Nassim Nicholas Taleb - Understanding uncertainty

Learning Philosophy

I believe that continuous learning is essential for staying current in the rapidly evolving field of data science. Beyond books, I actively follow academic papers, attend conferences, and engage with the data science community through various online platforms.

The intersection of theory and practice is where the most interesting insights emerge, so I'm always looking for ways to apply what I learn to real-world problems.
