Urban Heat Island Analysis

One of my most comprehensive data analysis projects examines temperature patterns across Dallas neighborhoods to understand urban heat island effects and their socioeconomic implications. This project combines geospatial analysis, statistical modeling, and data visualization to identify patterns and inform urban planning decisions.

The analysis revealed significant temperature variations across different neighborhoods, with correlations to factors such as tree canopy coverage, building density, and socioeconomic status. These insights have important implications for public health, energy consumption, and urban planning.
