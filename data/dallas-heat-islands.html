Dallas Urban Heat Islands Analysis

Analyzing temperature patterns and socioeconomic implications

Project Overview

This project analyzes temperature patterns across Dallas neighborhoods to understand urban heat island effects and their socioeconomic implications. Urban heat islands occur when urban areas experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to human activities and the built environment.

Methodology

Using a combination of satellite temperature data, census data, and geographic information systems (GIS), I mapped temperature variations across Dallas neighborhoods. The analysis includes:

  • Satellite thermal imagery processing
  • Spatial analysis of temperature patterns
  • Correlation with socioeconomic indicators
  • Statistical modeling of heat island intensity

Key Findings

The analysis revealed significant temperature disparities across Dallas neighborhoods, with some areas experiencing temperatures up to 10Â°F higher than surrounding areas. These heat islands often correlate with socioeconomic factors, highlighting environmental justice implications for urban planning.

Impact & Applications

This research contributes to understanding the relationship between urban development and climate impacts, providing data-driven insights for urban planning decisions. The findings can inform policies related to green space allocation, building materials, and community resilience planning.
